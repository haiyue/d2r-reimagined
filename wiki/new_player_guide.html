IMPORTANT:

Storing a large quantity of small items (Small Charms, Large Charms, Grand Charms, Jewels, Rings, Amulets, Belts, Etc.) in your Personal Stash and/or Horadric Cube in between game sessions (Save and Exit) can drastically increase the file size of your character which has a hardcoded limit of 8192 bytes on disk or around 8KB.

The file to watch is (charname).d2s in C:\Users\(your user)\Saved Games\Diablo II Resurrected\mods\Reimagined.

Keep smaller items(1x1 size) like jewels in Shared Tabs if using Personal tab for rune/orb stack storage. If you store Rune and Orb stacks in your personal tab, keep them stacked together, a stack of 100 quantity takes up less file space than 2 stacks of 1 quantity.

Failure to be aware of the file size limit and follow these basic guidelines will eventually result in items disappearing!

Never use the Horadric Cube for in between game storage, (Save and Exit), the cube contributes to your save file limit. Items disappear when the file size has reached the limit above.

How To Use The Item Upgrade System

Item Upgrades You Should Be Using

 

Characters

Max character level is 100, mercenaries can reach 99.

Skills cap out at 25 hard points, up from 20.

Many skills and their synergies have been changed, so experiment and try new things!

You can respec at any time by purchasing a Token of Absolution from potion vendors in each act.

 

Items

Check the website pages to look up runewords, sets and unique items.

Item names have been updated to show both their item level ( XX ) and quality:

  • [N] = Normal, [X] = Exceptional, [E] = Elite


Many miscellaneous items drop as 'stacks' (the + sign in the top-left is a visual indicator).
Stacks store up to 100 quantity at a time.

The stack drops pertain to non-DLC only, while they can still be made in DLC, items drop as singles for use in the new tabs.

 

You can purchase two utility items from Blacksmith vendors that allows you to retrieve socketed items from weapons and armor.

Rune Pliers work on Runewords and any other socketed items. Jewel Pliers only work on non-Runeword items.

Cube Recipes: Check the Cube Recipes Tab for a bunch of useful recipes to use while levelling!

Item Enchants: Items can be enchanted a number of times to add various properties. Check the Item Enchants Tab.

Item Crafting: You can craft powerful items to help you level, or max your character's potential! Check the Item Crafting Tab.

Twisted Essences have been replaced by Socketing Orbs.

Jewels can be used to 'socket-punch' items when you want a specific number of sockets (as long as the item supports it). 

Item Rarity = Jewel Rarity required to punch sockets with

  • Note that number of open sockets is dependent on the ilvl of the item

All Elite Helms can roll up to 4 sockets in Reimagined!

Melee Characters can obtain a Small Charm ( Collin's Might ) that makes your melee attacks deal splash damage 
The charm drops from Corpsefire in the Den of Evil on normal (once per game, and only if you don't have one in inventory already). 
It can be upgraded a number of times via cube recipes to deal more damage and have less penalties.

 

Quality of Life

Rune Stacks:

Cube a single rune by itself to convert it into a stack.

Cube a rune stack to create a single, useable rune.

You can upgrade two runes to a single rune of the next tier higher. 

Cube two single runes, or a rune stack (qty 2 or more), and a TP tome.

  • Reduces stack quantity by 2.
  • Your tome is also returned full!

You can downgrade a rune to one of the next tier lower. 

Cube a rune, or rune stack, with an ID Tome.

  • Reduces stack quantity by 1.
  • Your tome is also returned full!

 

Storage:

You can store all of your Gems in a gem bag. 

  • You can use the bag directly in lots of crating recipes without having to take gems out beforehand!

Up to 99 Gems can be added to a Gem bag at a time.

  • The Gem Bag can store up to 65,000 gems.
  • Gem grabbers are used to retrieve gems from the bag. Gem grabbers can be purchased from most vendors.

Infernal Keys can be stored in a Keychain, and extracted with the appropriate key grabber. 

  • Grabbers can be purchased at many vendors.

 

Gameplay Changes

Nightmare difficulty will feel the same until around mid Act 3 where the level starts to ramp up to accommodate the new Hell changes. By the end of Act 5 and killing Baal the area level is around 69-70 and Nightmare Terror Zone level cap is increased to 80 to facilitate farming before attempting to tackle Hell difficulty.

Nightmare Terror zones drop up to a CHAM rune.

Sunder charms can also be obtained from Nightmare terror zones.

Hell difficulty has been scaled up from the original game to encompass levels 90+ as well as terror zone level cap increased to 99. It is recommended to farm in NM TZ until 85+ and getting resistances situated and your build a goal before attempting Hell.

  • Monster HP is about 4X higher and damage is about 1.5X higher on all player counts.

Terror Zones changed to rotate between 5 sets. Each TZ rotation is Act based and contains most of the original TZs within that act. Cows are under Act 4 due to the limit on number of TZs allowed.

Cow Level Portal now only requires Wirt's Leg to open.

Countess rune drop table revised, highest rune is now:

  • Normal: THUL
  • Nightmare: KO
  • Hell: VEX
  • Countess Rune drop table has been extended to other Super Uniques:
    • Act 2 - Halls of the Dead: Bloodwitch the Wild
    • Act 3 - Ruined Temple: Battlemaiden Sarina
    • Act 4 - River of Flame: Hephasto (Countess Table +1, Normal: DOL, Nightmare: UM, Hell: LO)
    • Act 5 - Frozen River: Frozenstein (Countess Table +1, Normal: DOL, Nightmare: UM, Hell: LO)

See this page for more information.

Charm Weight System

There is a weight system to restrict the number of charms placed in the main inventory. If you reach charm weight 41 or more then your maximum mana is set to 0. You can check weight in the advanced stats panel. This applies to all Charms.

  • Small Charms have a weight of 1
  • Large charms have a weight of 2
  • Grand Charms have a weight of 3
