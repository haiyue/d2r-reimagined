1. INTRO

Bone Necro has some very welcomed added utility and dimention on Reimagined when compared to retail D2R, with the main aspect of it being the Energy synergy the bone spells have.

The best way currently to put that to use is with the Maadi set. The set gives high amounts of stats and Bone Spear skill levels, and ties everything with Energy Shield oSkill (that while it wont get you nowhere close to being chonky as a Sorc with it, still provides more than enough of a survival buffer on a class that already has plenty meatshields protecting it).

I am currently seeing about making Teeth better (and, consequently, also make other gear options more appealing to Bone Necros in the future patches), but that would be just another viable option to build the char. When/if that happens, I will probably also put together a gear profile for that.

Here we will focusing on making most of Bonespear and the Maadi set.

3. REASONS TO PLAY THIS BONE SPEAR NECRO

  1. Cool gear considerations and stat allocation juggling during leveling.
  2. Very interesting ‘glass cannon’ build-oriented in its mentality, but that is actually very safe.
  3. Very active playstyle.

 

4. REASONS TO NOT PLAY THIS BONE SPEAR NECRO

  1. Not the fastest clean. Even with 3 Spears, the spread of your damage is not that good, so you wont be screen clearing.
  2. Very slow start. The juggling between sustain and damage can be demanding during leveling as well.

 

5. MAIN STATS

Finally some subversion on the decades old D2 stat logic. You still put as much Strength you need to equip your item. You will put nothing into Dexterity and Vitality and everything else on Energy.

This is for endgame, ofc. During leveling, put as much Vitality you feel you need to be safe and sustain your Blood Casting (more on it later), and then put all you can in Energy, but don’t go overboard unless you are fine with it.

 

6. ENDGAME SKILL BUILD

For leveling, start with Teeth until you feel it starts dragging to much to kill stuff, then pivot to Spear. Currently Teeth damage absolutely does not hold at all at endgame, but it is decent enough to clear all normal and then some in NM, but don’t let it drag too much. With Teeth your boss killing is also quite bad, so pivoting to Spear early is good to help on that aspect. Bone Spear does that job well for all the regular gameplay, with Bone Spirit only pulling substantially ahead of Spear on single-target damage when you start stacking ludicrous amounts of Energy Synergy (like 700+ points).

Therefore, the endgame build I use is the following, for lvl 100 with all extra skill points:

  • 1 Teeth (Pre req only on a build trying to maximize Maadi and Bonespear)
  • 1 Corpse Explosion (Pre-req. Still a too good spell like ever, I just avoid using it cuz its lame)
  • 25 Bone Spear (Your bread and butter trash killer. It will end with numbers that are analogous to other magic builds single target tools, but it pierces and you can have up to 3. It wont have good spread to screen clean, but you will delete bunched up or lined up packs super fast.)
  • 25 Bone Spirit (Your single target tool once its damage starts to pull ahead. By the end, its damage is around 25% to 30% higher than Bone Spear)
  • 1 Bone armor (Another defensive layer, but since it provides no synergies to the damage skills, no way to put more than 1 point.)
  • 25 Bone Wall (Synergy for both you damage spells and Bone Armor, which is the reason we prio this along with Bone Prison instead of Teeth. These should be the last thing you point points into after the damage spells and your summons).
  • 25 Bone Prison (Synergy for both you damage spells and Bone Armor, which is the reason we prio this along with Bone Wall instead of Teeth. These should be the last thing you point points into after the damage spells and your summons)
  • 1 Weaken (You can put more at the start, but at the end, 1 point will get you to -100 skill cap effect)
  • 1 Clay Golem (Pre req and a good ‘meat’ shield)
  • 1 Iron Golem (Pre req, good meat shield and the carrier of an additional aura for you at the end)
  • 1 Blood Golem (Your most important golem. You need this one up at all times because it boosts your damage by A LOT. At the midgame until early post-hell gearing stages you might want to spend more points here to make it easier to keep them alive as they are very important, but then by the end 1 point will be enough for the 50% magic damage cap and to keep it alive comfortably)
  • 1 Golem Mastery (To improve your meatshields)
  • 1 Summon Resist (Same as above)
  • 1 Fire Golem (because at this point why not, another good meatshield).
  • 0-1 Revive (Will need 2 points into pre-reqs too – but no need for Skeleton Mastery as you get that from your boots. If you go with this, it will add up to 112 skill points, so you will need to cut 1 point from some other stuff or get them via Large Charms. In theory, this should be the optimal thing to do, as 3 points would be totally worth for 5 extra meatshields. I don’t use it because I HATE this skill and can’t wait to see it be yoinked from the game one day, but like I said, this would be optimal. If not using it, just put the last couple points into Teeth).

 

7. GEAR SETUP 

You are, of course, locked into using Maadi’s full set with this build. The set consists of Wand and Necro Head combo, helm, chestpiece and gloves. This leaves you with only belt, boots, jewelry and alternate weapon slots to customize.

Therefore, most of the things exposed here will be regarding gear customizatio (enchants and corruptions) and not selection. Just use whatever generalistic good caster gear you get during leveling.

 

7.1 MAADI’S PARADOX

The set bonuses grant you the biggest chunk of the set skill values, and also with some defensive tools in the form of flat life and cannot be frozen and Energy Shield oSkill. It also gives you resists, but those are somewhat less impactfull since your Energy Shield applies before them whenever you take damage. They are still important to be there if/when your EShield breaks down and you take damage directly into your life.

 

7.1.1. GLOVE SLOT – MAADI’S SILENCE

The gloves gives you some good PDR/MDR numbers, that work very well in tandem with Energy Shield (MDR in particular, since the PDR will most likely only come into play after Bone Armor breaks and you shouldnt be just standing there getting hit enough for it to happen).

The main bonuses of the piece are the damage buffs you get on the 2 and 3 piece sets.

For corruption, 10 all attributes is the optimal one here.

Regarding enchants, for leveling you can get life and LPK enchants to help your sustain, for endgame though the optimal enchants are 3x 10 Vit and Energy enchants.

 

7.1.2. ARMOR SLOT – MAADI’S SPIRIT

The piece is a heavy stat-oriented piece, both on its main stats and set bonuses. Also gives some mana for more ‘virtual hp’ and a nice chunk of MDR.

For corruption, the optimal one is 1 all skills, but the more attainable ones would be one of the defensive variants (indestructible and defense, or block chance/rating) or the life/mana variants (flat or 5% of each). Nothing of that much impact after 1 all skills.

For enchants, 5x 10 Vit and 15 Energy are the best ones for endgame.

 

7.1.3. HEAD SLOT – MAADI’S VISION

Generalist piece, both in the base stats and set. Provides some FHR, FRW, FCR, good chunk of Vitality and a few resists.

For corruption, the optimal one is 1 all skills, but this is another slot that can roll 10 all atributes, so that should be the more attainable goal.

For enchants, you can choose between 6 magic skills and 3 all skills. All skills may sound appealing at first glance as it also helps your golems and curse, but I would honestly just go for 6 magic skills all the time here as the extra 3 skill on the summons are barely felt at the end and you cap your curse effect anyways).

 

7.1.4. WEAPON SLOT – MAADI’S TORCH

Provides skills, 1 additional Bone Spear projectile and a WHOLE LOT of FCR. Only use the 2 all skills variants to try to corrupt, as that is the only roll that matters on the item so it can be aimed for quite easily.

For corruption, 1 all skill is the only one that matters here, but its not something I recommend farming for if you play fairly (I dont farm for those +1ask and never will xD). The FCR corruptions are not that bad while you are in the process of gearing as you might have sub-optimal rings and bad roll ranges on other pieces, but at the end you will overcap FCR most likely as Necro only needs 125, so they are not optimal too. The only other thing that gives some benefit is the MF/GF corrupt. Nothing else matters.

For enchants, 5x +2% magic damage -2% Enemy magic resist upgrades are a lock once you find the wand variant you are happy with.

 

7.1.5. SHIELD SLOT – MAADI’S SOUL

Another piece with skills, FCR and Spear Projectiles. It also has some good defensive bonus, that you wont put much to use with all your ‘boys’ running intercept for you.

For corruptions, once again, 1 all skills is optimal, but the more attainable ones would be one of the defensive variants (indestructible and defense, or block chance/rating) or the life/mana variants (flat or 5% of each). Nothing of that much impact after 1 all skills.

For enchants, 5x 10 Vit and 12 Energy are the best ones for endgame.

 

7.2. BELT SLOT – WISDOM’S WRAP (OR GHEED’S WAGER)

A well rolled Gheed’s is the optimal choice at the start, when you might still need extra FCR to hit the 125% breakpoint, and still benefits a lot from -enemy magic resist.

Later though, you can be FCR capped without it and you will have overflowing -enemy magic res with your Weaken curse, so I prefer Wisdom’s Wrap for the up to 60 energy and garanteed 1 skills and no other variable stat that matters much, which makes rolling for good corruption on this item super easy. But if you get a God-roll Gheeds and good corruption, by all means use it instead.

For corruption, once again 1 all skills is best, but this slot can roll +10 all attributes. Coupled with how easy it is to have a good Wisdom Wrap version to slam a corruption on, this slot is prone to be farmed for good corruption.

For enchants, 3x 10 Vit and Energy are the best ones for endgame.

 

7.3. BOOT SLOT – MARROWWALK

This ones are so sexy. They give a lot of FRW, some dex for block (very low impact tbh) and some additional STR to equip the rest of your gear easier. But honestly, the only thing you should focus on this piece is it being 2 necro skills and not 1. This once again leads to a very easy slot to fish for the perfect corruption.

Since you get teleport from other source in endgame, there is no reason to not fish for your favorite corruption. Too bad this slot cant roll +10 all attributes.

For enchants, 3x 10 Vit and Energy are the best ones for endgame.

 

7.4. JEWELRY SLOTS

The range of jewelry, specially regarding rare and crafted options is absurdly huge on this mod.

Barring absurd crafted and rare rolls, which I wont consider since I can’t ever know what you can get, I will keep my suggestions to Unique and Set options.

This is in my opinion the best combination for endgame. A +2 Foul Sigil with high roll on attributes is in my opinion the best piece you can get for amulet on the unique/set rarity range – it also gives you a good chunk of Poison res, the only one that still matters with EShield active. For rings, Jordan Sigils give you the FCR you need to cap it, 2 skills, and a huge boost to mana to improve your EShield ‘virtual life’ angle.

For corruptions, 1 all skills again on all these slots. The more attainable ones would be +10 attributes on all 3 slots as well.

For enchants, 2 necro skills is locked on the neckpiece. For rings, 2x 6 all attributes are the best damage option, but life/LPK are the best ones for sustain. See which you prefer, as the per-enchant impact on Energy on this slot is very reduced compared to everything else.

7.4.1. ALTERNATIVE OPTIONS FOR JEWELRY

For variant rings, you have Sling, the new ROTW addition. The ring is SUPER good. It has variable FCR and -enemy magic resistance numbers, as well as up to 20 Energy. It gives ‘only’ 1 magic skills though, instead of 2 all skills and absurd mana numbers when compared to Jordan’s Sigil. In the end I went with Jordan over this just because it felt like a more generally good choice that balanced good damage, with larger mana pool and some boost to the summon, but the choice here is A LOT CLOSER than it was with the belts for example. Sling is clear cut choice before you have surplus of -enemy resist, and arguably can still be a lot better if you dont feel you need Jordan’s extra mana. If it werent for EShield, I would use this one over Jordan EASILY. And I’m still not 100% sure on my choice here because this one is honestly very good.

Less powered but still functional options, you have stuff like Plantar’s Enlightment, Faerie Rings and Fellowship’s Hope that you can make very good use while gearing for the best pieces.

For amulet, instead of Foul’s Sigil (that can have some pretty bad rolls), you can use a Mara’s Kaleidoscope on this slot. The old trusty amulet also gives 2 skills, but gives less possible ceiling on attributes and more spread out resist numbers. Lucky Sigil is a good one if you want more MF/GF.

Another option is also swapping one ring along with the neck slot to use 2-piece Lords of Hell set (Diablo’s Soulstone Ring and Baal’s Cryptic Amulet). With the ring giving 1 skill and the neck 2 skills, and the set bonus another 2 skills, these two items add up to 5 all skills (the bigges value you can get by a amulet + 1 ring combo in game if you dont take the random roll item sinto account), as well giving you life, mana, big mana regen, BIG MDR numbers on the ring and some physical res. Truly a VERY good combination of items that I use on a lot of chars when I’m searching for the optimal endgame things.

 

7.5. WEAPON SWAP SLOT

On alternate slot, for this build the uncontested swap is Warrior Untamed and not CtA. The extra defense buff make your summons be even more tanky and thats their function on this build. They aint gonna be dealing much damage.

For shield,  go for a Savant Sin for Holy Shield. Uptier it so it can have 4 slots (Its a Scutum base).

1 all skills corrupts would be the only thing that would matter on these, so I wouldnt care much. If you happen to get, awesome, if not, eh.

 

7.6. JEWELS

7.6.1. UNIQUE JEWELS

The best jewels you have are the Heaven Facets. Even with them not giving life and mana anymore, they are the best jewels you can get, so slap them on all 12 slots you have available (4 head, 4 armor, 3 shield, 1 weapon) and if you can, on your alternate weapon slots too (another 7). And your merc will also want to use some (12 in total), so you are in course to farm a measly 31 Heaven Facets for this build. Haha.

During leveling and early gearing, using LUM runes is actually SUPER good on this build. The 10 Energy adds up quite well when used on all slots, and give you a good amount of damage as you progressively swap for Heaven Facets as you farm them.

7.6.2. UBER COLOSSAL JEWEL

For you, you obviously want the magic jewel (Guardian’s Light). For your merc, use the physical one (Protector’s Stone).

 

7.7. CHARMS

Almost all unique charms are good here. The ones that give you +skills are the main target ones and the ones you dont have a reason to skip. The other ones (particularly the small charms) give you good life and mana ratio per slot, so they are not bad to use if you dont have better alternatives - but aim to replace them with better magic large charms eventually. 

Lie & Death is awesome, and you mitigate its downsides in several ways on the build - so when you get to the point of farming Tristram Ubers, do it untill you can get a +2 all skills one if you dont have it already. Of the unique large charms that boost your stats by a percentage, the only one I’d use is Valknut, for the up to 10% bonus energy. Ogre's additional 20% strength is decent at the start, but after you get a bunch of 10 stats corruption going you eventually can equip all your gear with no points into Str at all, so the charm loses the only benefit that is good enough to justify using it.

Poison and Bone skillers are decent, specially if you can get them with some good other stats. But for me, they get outscalled pretty easily by large charms with Bone Spear or any other useful skills (like one with Revive to free you from 2 pre-rew points, or one with Weaken, or Fire Golem or any other combination of factors that let you save points that you can revert to other synergy points).

Bone Spear LCs in particular are worth using even if only +1 to skill and low numbers of Energy and Vitality (these attributes will always roll together with skills on large charms). For other skills, you need a good combination of skill and attributes (3 skill LCs can roll something like 8-12 of each atribute along with the skill, which converts into a lot of damage in the end). You can also use some -enemy res LC at the start, but I dont see a reason to do it towards the end.

For sunder, you really don't need one to play. Magic immune is not something that exists basically. You can have a Renewed sunder charm though for extra %magic damage or -enemy magic res if you dont mind having some -magic res yourself (which you really shouldn't mind, as you are a EShield build after all). I have a good 14% magic damage one crafted, but it wasnt on this char when I took the screenshots (landed it to Blessed Hammer pally).

 

7.8. IRON GOLEM

During regular gameplay, Iron Golem is little more than a glorified corpse to facetank stuff for you. At the end though, when you can reliably keep them alive and dont respec much anymore, they can be good tools to grant you some very impactful effects, mostly preferably auras (as for ctc effects their attack speed tend to be too slow, specially on a build with no Fanaticism aura).

Our merc already covers mostly everything we want, as in Meditation, Cleansing, Sanctuary, Blind and Dim Vision procs due to Blindsight...(more on him below).

So the 2 choices I feel have some good benefits for you would be:

  1. Holy Freeze made from a Doom runeword (best level for a single item + some procs), or;
  2. Salvation aura from a Holy Aura armor set item (it doesnt benefit you that much, but it does wonders for your summons and merc).

You could also juggle merc items around and use some other stuff on your Iron Golem, thats up to you.

Pick your favorite and roll with it.

 

8. MERCENARY

For this one, not in a million years you would convince me to not use an A2 Prayer merc. I used them even before they had a good Sanctuary option available.

You could use a full Prayer-runeword A5 merc to attain up to 7 ticks of lvl 61 Prayer instead of an A2, but honestly, I don’t use that because it just feels SUPER lame and totally something you can only do because Prayer runeword is something that goes under the radar so much I didnt care enough to make a fuzz about (oops). Also, no reason to do a Sanctuary stacking A5 merc as I dont feel you need much more -enemy magic res than what you already have.

Below I will present a quick gear setup I put together for him, but wont go into too much details. I want his prayer ticks to be as high as possible, and for him to also have good aura-stacking benefits. So, same thing regarding corruptions, 1 all skill is the best on all slot, but I settled for anything marginally useful. Most of my mercs I dont even go for corruptions, only enchants.

A2 Prayer Merc:

  • Helm: Blindsight. 3x all skill enchant.
  • Weapon: Spirit Light. This one gives your merc up to lvl 15 Sanctuary (-25 enemy magic res), which compliments what you already have pretty well as it doesnt ‘cost’ anything to have. The weapon has good damage and also gives your Merc lvl 12 Chilling Armor, so its an allstar here.
  • Chest: Will of the Zakarum. All in PDR% enchants. Whatever beneficial corruption. 4x Heaven Facets. This armor grants Meditation, which is the vehicle for another prayer ping, and also makes your mana regen ABSURDLY fast which is awesome if you ever happen to get hit with EShield up.
  • Gloves: Lachdanan’s. It gives 2 all skills and some good offensive stats. Decent enough for a merc focusing all skills. If you can get an Iron Skin corrupt, perfect. You can also use a Steelrend here if you think your summons having acess to Thorns does some work, but I didnt care for it.
  • Belt: Cortex Wrap (Sine's Psionics). Aim for high absorbs. Since you will use the set’s boots, this belt will give your merc elemental absorbs you lack on not using a Zero Effect boots but it also gives you 1 all skills. If you dont care about the balanced approach, you can just use a Ogre’s Embrace or a Kashya’s Ward for more defensive range here.
  • Boots: Phasewalker Boots (Sine's Psionics). Aim for lvl 5 Cleansing Aura here, not much else to care about. It is the vehicle for another Prayer ping.
  • Rings: 1x Bul-Katho's for 1 all skill, flat life and Leech (that is lacking on this gear setup). 1x Diablo’s Soulstone Ring to pair up with the amulet and have lots of skills.
  • Amulet: Baal’s Cryptic Amulet to pair with the ring for high skills. You can also use a Sequence of Seasons here if you think your summons having acess to lvl 1 Thorns does some work, but I didnt care for it.

OBS: Before you have your mercenary at lvl 99, you will most likely have to slap a Hel rune in your weapon or get some stat points (Dexterity in particular) on some item to be able to equip the Spirit Light spear as it has 193 Dex requirement.

 

9. GAMEPLAY LOOP

Regular gameplay loop consists of:

  • Buff up with Battle Command.
  • Check which set gives you the better golems.
  • Summon them in the respective best sets.
  • Rebuff BC and Buff Battle Orders and Shout to buff you and all your fwends.
  • Buff Holy Shield.
  • Swap to main setup.
  • Buff Energy Shield.
  • Go out into the wild and mash stuff. If you sue revives, get your preferred ones and re-buff them.
  • Kill stuff by using Weaken first (if you feel you need, as you will one shot stuff with spear regardless at the very end with how big its numbers are. it will affect a couple of screens away by the end) and them Spearing/Spiriting them.

 

10. MECHANICS

10.1. BLOOD GOLEM

Blood golem is your most important summon. It provides 2 layers of benefits you need to have to make the build functional.

First one is Bloodcasting. The golem provides you with an aura that makes your skills cost health instead of mana. While that can (and should imo) be abused by any necro build, its impact is mostly felt on Bone Necros due to the higher rate of spells being cast and those spells also having their cost balanced around this mechanic. The golem itself provides you with a way to offset this cost, by also having a Life per Kill effect on the aura. Most of the time wont be enough to offset the costs entirely, so you will need extra sources of health regen and LPK while you level. At the end though, with your Blood Golem supercharged by all the +ask items you have and your mercenary also dishing out big ticks of Prayer healing, you shouldn’t need much besides of what the build passively gives you.

Second benefit is the golem aura also increases magic spell damage. On versions past the aura gave a different effect that would limit its potential to the specific necro spells but currently, it is just flat %magic damage. 

I only saw this changed when writing this guide, as I assumed there was no reason to have changed this effect as I always thought it working for other classes would be too busted, but I was surprised it did. I'm even more surprised other people haven't been abusing this thing on other magic skill casters (Abyss Warlock in particular, that can wield a 2-handed weapon with Fire and Blood Golem oSkill and FREAKING 20 Golem Mastery that can have double %magic enchants…).
