1. INTRO

With the A3 mercenary enchant remakes and all the CTC options going around, I’m in a spiral of just making cool chars that put those things to use.

I done several versions of Shockwave druids that abuse the fact that the spell procs a lot of CTC that honestly feel kinda abusive so I get tired kinda fast. But this one I’m presenting here I liked because it feels a lot more “fair” and “intentional” while still being super strong.

The synergized Fissure procs are strong and noticeable, but due to the nature of how they work (several Fissure procs dont stack on the same floor tile when they occur), they don’t overlap. That way they feel like a competent suplementary damage source instead of just go balls to the walls blasting everything in sight the same way a Shockwave druid focused on proccing Winter’s Pulse or Infernal Orb would (for example).

This lead me down the road of trying to make the char also have a good physical melee angle to it instead of only mindless stacking some elemental skill damage and just playing like a Fire/Arctic Claw druid on steroids while abusing something I don't think is fully intentional.

The main part of my liking for this build is that it feels like a true 3-way hibrid, which perfectly fits the druid class fantasy: part shapeshifter, part summoner, part elemental spells.

3 times the fun!

3. REASONS TO PLAY THE ARMAGEDDON BEAR

  1. True hibrid. This build offers everything you like in druids: zooming around as a ball of fur smacking stuff around, having furry friends, and a lot of spells with super dramatic effects that do less damage than they look like at first glance. xD
  2. Cool damage profile juggling a few axis of scaling.
  3. Putting a cool item like Earth Shifter to work.
  4. Putting A3 mercs to work after so much time of neglect.

 

4. REASONS TO NOT PLAY THE ARMAGEDDON BEAR

  1. You dislike having to stack high amounts of Maul charges and maintaining the buff during gameplay (specially if you go for low level Lycanthropy variations);
  2. You are lame and think being 100% optimal is the only way to play.

 

5. MAIN STATS

Regular decades-old D2 stat logic. The bare minimum you need to equip gear on STR, whatever you feel you need on DEX for attack rating. After you go for some physical damage upgrades on your weapon, you really dont need much Dex for AR purposes.

 

6. ENDGAME SKILL BUILD

This guide gonna be light on leveling tips. The build honestly works just in multiple ways you decide to level. You can focus more on the melee part and let Maul + Shockwave do the clean up job with a heavy weapon setup. Or you can go more dogs and Armagedon and play more like a summoner/caster hibrid. Or if you don’t care about leveling as a semblance of the char you will eventually be, you can just go full blast summoner untill the point you feel like pivoting into the build.

For lvl 100 with all extra skill points, this is the build to use:

  • 1 Werebear (Your form of choice. Not Worth to spend more points in it since its ED% benefits are lower than every other physical skill and its defensive bonuses are worse than lycanthropy)
  • 25 Lycanthropy (Best defensive skill you got. If you ever feel you are good enough on the survival part and wants fore damage, you can drop points here and slap into something else like Shockwave or some other elemental spell for Armageddon/Doggos/Fissure synergies)
  • 25 Armageddon (Your first pseudo-passive source of damage and synergy for Dire Wolves and Fissure)
  • 25 Summon Dire Wolf (Your second pseudo-passive source of damage and synergy for Armageddon and Fissure. This also makes your A3 Merc Enchant gains more value)
  • 25 Maul (Your main source of physical damage, both in the charge-stacking and Shockwave Synergy. Its also what fix your attack speed problem entirely.)
  • 1 – X Shockwave (Your vehicle of destruction. You only really need a single point on it. As long as you mind your attack rating needs, all the damage necessary will be injected into the spell due to Maul synergy and ED% Maul stacks. At the end levels, put all extra points you have at it.)
  • 1 Oak Sage (Totally worth one point, if you have problems keeping this alive when you have a low amount of +skills, you can either spend more points on it temporarily or go the complete other way, just not use it until it has a less steep upkeep cost.)
  • 1 Heart of Wolverine (Totally worth one point, if you have problems keeping this alive when you have a low amount of +skills, you can either spend more points on it temporarily or go the complete other way, just not use it until it has a less steep upkeep cost.)
  • 1 Spirit of Barbs (Do not skip on this one. If you feel it is bad to maintain at lower +all skills, then spend more points on it until you can make do with only 1 point. Do no skip using it. You need its multi-elemental debuff to make your physical attacks and fire spells be effective.)
  • 1 Summon Grizzly Bears (With one point they are chonky and sponge a lot of damage. They also deal good damage once you stack good amounts of skills. This also makes your A3 Merc Enchant gains more value)
  • 0 – 1 Summon Spirit Wolves (you don’t have points to spare, but when you have enough + all skills they get chonky enough that you dont have to recast them all the time. Their base damage is still low, but they can be just 5 extra summons applying Enchant damage which is totally worth 1 point)
  • 0 – X Cyclone Armor (If you can be arsed to keep applying it, in theory its a buffer for elemental damage in engagements. You numbers will be like 1.2k absorb in normal build-case, and those numbers are not subject to resists, so a weak elemental fart wears the shield off. I don’t bother using at all, but the option is there for whoever wants it.)
  • 0 – 1 Hurricane (Very low passive ‘free’ damage that applies some slow and sometimes stagger mobs. If you don’t mind the upkeep, definitely worth 1 point)

 

7. GEAR SETUP 

Below I’ll show the gear profile I’m working with, and the reasoning for the choices I made. Due to the amount of items that exist in the mod, you could for sure achieve similar builds with different combinations of items/corrupts/enchants, so use this as inspiration to build your own.

The build does go for a lot of mix and match to achieve what it wants, but bonuses to all skills is what glues everything together. Bear builds tend to benefit more from +skill than your regular physical skill due to all the ‘dips’ it gets from it (Shockave 15% per level, Maul 30% every two levels, Bear form 5% per soft point). And then you have your summons (that have a substantial growth curve on raw skill levels) and Armageddon to sparkle it up at the top.

After that, you need to focus on secondary stats to a degree to make your damage sources effective. Crushing Blow and Deadly strike are needed to maximize the physical aspect of the build and -enemy fire/%fire damage for the Armageddon, Fire Enchant, Fissure procs and Dire Wolves damage (that benefit a lot from your %elemental damage. But not from your -enemy res. More on it in the mechanics chapter).

You work by adding a lot of minor damage sources into something cohesive, and not by hyperfocusing in one of them and maximizing that alone.

 

7.1. HELM SLOT – SPIRIT OF THE LAND

This one was the choice I made due to pure chonk. There were a few options that leaned more towards damage, but I felt this was the perfect slot to commit to letting go of a bit of damage to try to make up a bit of HP pool, PDR%, a hefty amount of attributes and even some magic resist on top of it all.

I think 3 all skill upgrades is the best to do here. 6 fire spells increase your Armageddon and Wolves a lot more, but 3 all skills also benefit your Spirits, Bears, and most important, also your Shapeshift skills.

7.1.1. ALTERNATIVE OPTIONS FOR HELM

If you want even more PDR% and Def, you can use something like a Stone Feather, but I think you sacrifice too much of everything else doing that.

For heavy damage skews, Gillian’s Tiara is probably the best option. Cerebu’s Bite or Jalal’s could also be options if you want to close up some Attack Rating to shore up some numbers deficit. Even a Sanctuary Scion might be na option depending on the 2 skills it happens to roll.

But honestly, I don’t like ANY of the current Druid helms for pure or hibrid heavy physical damage skewed builds. Which is why I felt particularly fine in reserving this slot for a heavy defensive piece like Spirit of the Land.

 

7.2. GLOVE SLOT – ELEMENTAL OR PHYSICAL CRAFT

I’m not one of the biggest fans of Crafted gear because they feel a bit too ubiquitous for my tastes, particularly in hybrid builds - and since as those are usually the kind of chars I like to do, they are always there. I try to avoid crafts for most of the gameplay, and I will present a few alternative options below, but for this build I ended up using elemental crafts more than usual (4 items when I usually try to use 2 at max when I can). 

Physical crafts can be equally effective to acomplish the same goal too, but I had a lot of Elemental crafts left over from other chars that I could use up on this char after I beat Ubers with it so I took the chance.

The glove pair below I picked up because 1) it was the one that a decent corruption landed (lost so many good crafts to bad corruption luck) and 2) extra AR and 3) having CBF allowed me not to worry about trying to find CBF in another piece, which freed me up a lot on the other builds.

Talking about corruptions, grab whatever good corruption you are able to. 10% CB+DS, 5% elemental damage /-5% enemy fire res +FCR are the best ones probably. For enchants, first see what you will get with the rest of the crafts on your gear since sometimes you can get enough of some stats there. In my case, I ended up doing 3x Deadly Strike upgrades on my gloves because I had too little (aka, nothing) of that.

7.2.1. ALTERNATIVE OPTIONS FOR GLOVES

Lachdanan’s is a good option. It gives up to 2 skills, which add to good amounts of all physical, elemental and summon damage for the build; some good amount of flat AR, flat weapon damage and ED to demons.

If you prefer to focus more on the elemental aspect, Burning Souls would probably be the best choice. Although, Hellmouth Gloves proc like MAD on the build, and end up adding A LOT more damage than you would expect initially with such low level CTC procs. 

But, at the end of the day, these option are just sidestepping and losing against good elemental crafts in the case you just don’t wanna have to spend the time it takes to get a good one.

 

7.3. ARMOR SLOT – SHADOWTRICK OR ARKAINE'S VALOR

These feel almost equivalent for the tool at hand. Arkaine’s is potentially 1 extra skill point and 5% extra elemental damage and a lot more HP so it would be my first choice most of the time.

So why would I choose Shadowtrick over it?

Well, due to the nature of the gearing used on this setup, I could use some more -res on top of what I could muster with enchants, jewels and Barbs. Aditionally, the -% defense Conviction provides also helps you a lot (specially at the start before you get good AR numbers), and GREATLY assist the summons as they have low AR too (the merc enchant also do wonders here).

Arguments could be made for using Arkaine’s on yourself and Shadowtrick on the mercenary (specially since on my gear setup I ended up using both those armor just the other way around), but I prefer slapping the Shadowtrick on myself because of a simple reason: your Wolves and Bears will be in the forefront of the fight more often along with you, while your mercenary (A3 Fire) will tend to be behind – which means that might incurr in some enemies not being affected by Conviction when they would.

Regarding corruptions use whatever best you can. I fell into the rabit-hole of trying to force and Indestructible Ethereal version as I had piles of Shadowtricks lying around, but I honestly don’t recommend putting yourself through if you are not absurdly lucky or doesnt care about your sanity.

For upgrades, since Lycan gives Bear form a good amount of PDR% and I went with Spirit of the Land as helm, I felt safe foregoing of the more usual (to me) PDR% or %HP life enchant I do on chests and went with 5x 3all res/1max all res enchants. Since you dont wield a shield on this build, being able to get some max res on the chest is very welcomed.

7.3.1. ALTERNATIVE OPTIONS FOR CHEST ARMOR

If you have a good Diablo Scale laying around, you can pretty much use them in place of Arkaine’s if you feel like. I still don’t feel its the best course of action, but theres a lot of combinations of items you could make to justify it.

A crafted piece that rolls with extra skills on top of the fixed mods could be justifiable too depending on the other stuff you happen to get.

Finally, I don’t feel like going for heavy physical damage options like Lachdanan’s or Tyrael’s, because by that point you might just start considering being a regular Shockwave build instead, but again, I can never know what combination of items can bring you to wanna use something.

 

7.4. BELT SLOT – OGRE'S EMBRACE

The second chonk allstar on the build. The piece gives you a lot of Armor (innate and with Iron Skin, also, make it Ethereal) PDR% and Vitality, and also Strength to equip your weapon and armor, which ends up converting in even more Vit at the end.

I chose to go with some flat life and mana enchants on my copy, to put all those %hp bonuses you have as a druid to use, but I think all stats and resists enchants are also super good here. For corruption, pick anything useful.

7.4.1. ALTERNATIVE OPTIONS FOR BELT

I find kinda hard to justify changing this slot since its so easy to get, and for melees that use a lot of Strength Ogre’s Embrace is just a blessing; but you could use some other stuff here – specially if you happen to get an Iron Skin corruption on your gloves.

Dreadfury belt that happens to roll a useful skill for you is a potential upgrade over elemental crafts if you wanna to focus the fire damage of your gear more. The hits causing monsters to flee is not something I like any item having, but most of the time you will be one-shotting stuff if you are pushing very high levels of gear, and the ones you dont, are usually imunne. So that could be an option for the very end.

Again, I would just use Ogre’s Embrace and call it a day.

 

7.5. BOOT SLOT – CRAFTED ELEMENTAL OR ZERO EFFECT

Differently from crafted gloves, where you get good amounts of Crushing Blow innately on the physical Craft, the boots only give you faster movement. So if you gonna use a Craft here, use Elemental ones.

Zero Effect though are my recommendation for most of the game. Even at the end you need to have a pretty good alternative to justify dropping Zero Effect boots, SPECIALLY on melee chars. I ended up doing that because I the char honestly feels kinda impervious by this point, and I got enough PDR% and CBF from other sources.

I once again stole my trusted pair of booties with Burst of Speed from my other char.

For enchants, I would go with Vitality + Strength enchants to chain-equip the rest of your gear (the boots will most likely give you enough str to equip Ogre’s Embrace, that will allow you to equip everything else). There is nothing that much to use on this slot in particular and thats why I tend to go for this enchant, but if you prefer something else, by all means. %HP (and %Mana) do have big numbers on this slot...

7.5.1. ALTERNATIVE OPTIONS FOR BOOTS

If its not Zero Effect or Elemental Crafts, I honestly don’t see much of a reason to use anything else on this slots. You could maybe slap some Stalion Hooves here, but then again, at this point I would just play a straight Shockwave build then.

 

7.6. WEAPON SLOT – EARTH SHIFTER

Using this weapon is the reason to do the build. It has very decent melee physical damage (250-300% ED range), decent melee secondary stats (20% CB and 30-40% IAS), HUGE boost to fire damage (both in %fire damage and -enemy fire res), up to 5 shapeshifting skills (which do add to a good physical chunk of ED% damage like discussed before). As a cherry on top, it has a decent enough proc that synergizes very well with the rest of the build (it wont blow anything away, but at this point its honestly free damage).

There is a lot you can do to pimp this weapon even before touching the unique jewels you can use in it (which will be discussed more in the proper chapter), there are a lot of ways you can go about making it better.

Since its natural rolls have a huge field of variance, you will have to approach your choice of item kinda hollistically: honestly, corrupt a bunch of them, and grab the one that feels like its the best one, haha. I chose mine because ithad good rolls on basically everything, and got a decent corruption (albeit not one of the best corruptions, I think) in the form of 50% ED and 250 AR. Then I slapped 5 ED% + AR enchant on it, taking care not to lose value overflowing too much (or at all) over the 511% ED cap. Then, I completed the remaining enchant with +2/-2 fire enchants.

After that, make it ethereal. Then you can either slap a Zod rune on it (which is what I do, it feels more “fair” and I can just forget about it). Or you can always pay attention to its durability and use the cube recipe to repair it.

7.6.1. ALTERNATIVE OPTIONS FOR WEAPONS

For endgame, no reason to use anything else unless you want to drastically change the build from a true heavy melee damage/fire hybrid to something skewing more towards full CTC proc build. If you do that, stuff like Elder Tojanida (or even Frost Wyrms and changing the entire thing to be cold-based) and using good elemental shields like crafted or cry of the wretched (or chill of winter for cold-ctc) would be your choices. But since this is not we are doing here, just use your Earth Shifter.

For leveling with in-build flavor: Earthshaker, Groundshatter, Todesfaelle Flamme, Sunblighter and other weapons of the sort are pretty decent options.

 

7.7. JEWELRY SLOTS

The range of jewelry, specially regarding rare and crafted options is absurdly huge on this mod. Once again elemental crafts are the theoretical bis here. You can roll some absurdly broken stuff with enough resources. But you can live just fine with alternatives.

For amulet I think it is a bit easier, I tend to use Sigil of Hope a lot more than most people - I think the amulet is just so well rounded it just snuggles perfectly into whatever I build.

It provides up to 5% max res and 2 all skills and a whole ton of resists which are just generically good in everything. It gives flat AR that can be put to use very well by this build too. The corpse explosion proc is not absurdly good, but it doesnt hurt, and, specially on fire-based build that can proc it, its funny to look at the huge damage that goes out.

For rings, I chose to go with a couple of left-over elemental crafts I has left over that I got good corruptions on.

Enchant for rings once again with anything you feel you lack. Stuff like PDR%, IAS, Stats, HP, resists... everything is good. For amulet, druid skills.

7.7.1. ALTERNATIVE OPTIONS FOR JEWELRY

If you don’t like Sigil and/or dont wanna go through the bore of getting good elemental crafts, there are a few Options.

For amulets, Rising Sun can add up to a lot of damage on the build. For rings, lacking good elemental Craft rings, you can use Dwarf Stars as a decent sub in. These are Options focusing more on the fire damage side of things.

A +2 skills Highlords for amulet and Jackal’s and Bul-Katho’s are decent enough options too with more physical leaning stats.

 

7.8. WEAPON SWAP SLOT

On alternate slot, for this build the uncontested swap is Warrior Untamed and not CtA. Battle Cry would be a decent enough debuff to use on this build if you could use it, but ever since Battle Cry was locked out of being usable in shapeshift forms, there is no reason at all to equip it on shapeshifters anymore. Enchant it with 10% fire damage (explanation below) and if you can get a 5% elemental damage corrupt or 1 all skill, that is awesome too.

For shield, the best option would be having a 15% Elemental Crafted shield along with your Warrior's Untamed. I noticed that by the very endgame (when you have your alternate slot stuff filled with Heaven Facets while your main set weapon will have all Fire Facets) you might come to a gear point where you prefer to summon your dogs/bears/spirits while using the offset, not the main one. In those cases, having the offset gear also have %fire damage will improve the wolf numbers numbers even more.

 

7.9. JEWELS

7.9.1. UNIQUE JEWELS

Once you start amassing Unique Jewels, re-roll them using the 50 gem credit recipe until you get good ones.

The order of priority for them are, in my opinion Heaven Facets > Burnt/Summer Facets > Diamond Facets (at start, when you need some chonkyness) or any other melee-oriented jewels.

Put the Heaven Facet primarily on the Armor and Head slots, because they stay with you when you swap to your alternate buffing setup, and put the Burnt/Summer and Uber jewels on your weapon (and shield in case you use a variant build that has that) first. You can also, at the very very very end slap Heaven Facets on your alternate setups too, to increase your buffs even more. But I would only do that after both you and you merc are all pumped with the best jewels.

In the end, I finished the character with 8 Heaven Facets (on helm and body armor), and I put 4 perfect +5/-5 fire facets on my weapon, to garantee a decent amount of -enemy res (other 2 slots had a Zod and Phys colossal jewel). You can go with more melee jewels, in particular the ones that give CB and DS as you dont have much of those stats, but you might feel more short on -fire res if you do that than you might feel good having.

Also, don't be afraid of slapping Amethysts and Shael runes on your weapon if you need Attack Rating and Speed at the start of the gearing process.

7.10.2. UBER COLOSSAL JEWELS

There are 2 options that work on this build, Protector’s Stone (physical) and Defender’s Fire (fire).

In my opinion the best one to go for here is the physical gem. -Physical res is a very valuable stat that doesnt exist in those many slots, and the added ED% and flat damage the jewel provide are huge. Use it on your weapon though, as if it is elsewhere it will incur in the ED Bug.

But most importantly, I avoided the fire jewel because it has CTC on struck chance to give you a lvl 40 Enchant. That can override your mercenary enchant, which will 100% sure be stronger than it, and the merc will not re-apply theirs unless the jewel one drops. You could otherwise disable enchant from working on procs for items if you don’t care for it in any build, or delete the property of the jewel in Gild’s editor too if you want to use it.

I would just go with the physical one on this build.

 

7.10. CHARMS

Honestly, all unique charms are good here. Every single one of them give you good benefits, and the +skill ones (+2 Queen’s Call and +2 Life & Death) are particularly good, alongside Annihilus and Obsidian Beacon.

For sunders, I use both Fire and Physical sunders. The more important one is the physical one, if you have to chose for some reason, but I’d say use both.

If you get enough surplus resistances that you don’t mind using the retail version of the renewed sunder charms, they can provide a lot of damage with their % damage and -res stats.

I completed the rest of my charm weight with various 5% fire damage large charms. I think that they end up being better than any plain skiller you could use slot-by-slot. With good secondary on skillers they could definetely be better than %fire lcs, but I didnt got any decent ones lying around.

9. GAMEPLAY LOOP

Regular gameplay loop consists of:

  • Buff up with Battle Command.
  • Check which set gives you the better wolves and which give you the best every other summon.
  • Summon them in the respective best sets.
  • Rebuff BC and Buff Battle Orders and Shout to buff you and all your fwends.
  • Go into Bear form. On the set that gives you higher skill levels on Bear Form and Lycan.
  • Swap to main setup.
  • Go out into the wild and mash stuff with Maul till you fully buffs it. At the endgame it takes some time, cuz you will have a lot of charges.
  • Start Tec Slamming stuff out of existence. Don't let Maul drop.
  • Keep Armageddon rolling. It does last a decente amount of time, but don’t let it drop.

Due to how long it takes to build Maul charges up, the char is not at its full power on quick run-in farming sessions, but when running entire act TZs so you don't have to rebuild Maul charges.

 

10. MECHANICS

10.1. ENCHANT

Enchant has multiple layers of “stuff” going on. 

Enchant double dips masteries bonus. What is meant by that? Every source of elemental damage that is applied to a character that can attack is multiplied once by the WIELDER'S %damage on those elements. These sources also get multiplied first by similar bonuses on the CASTER'S gear beforehand, and if you are both the Caster and the Wielder, thas when you have the double dip.

This means for this build that the mercenary's enchant number, whatever it is, when applied to you, also gets multiplied again. So, your if your merc is a fire variant, when he enchants you for lets say 5k fire damage, and you have 130% fire damage on your gear, you get 11.5k fire damage added to your attacks. This is the actual damage you are applying on an attack (ping 1 single target).

Now there is the Splash Charm layer of shenanigans:

The splash charm works by getting your physical damage with no Skill ED applied (you dont add the Shockwave ED% damage on the splash charm effect, but I do think you add might add the Maul charge ED% because that works a bit differently. I’m not 100% sure whatsoever though), but you do add up all your elemental weapon damage (aka. all your enchant damage and other elemental damage in gear) to your splash charm radius. This hits your main target (ping 2 single target) as well as stuff on the aoe (ping 1 AOE).

THEN you have the Shockwave layer:

All your elemental damage (most relevantly your Enchant and flat damage from gear, facets and charms) are carried by Shockwave, albeit they are not multiplied by anything anymore at this point. This also hits your main target (ping 3 on single target) and then stuff in the Shockwave aoe (ping 2 AOE).

Stuff like this happen for every attack modifier (like the fire damage added to attack by Holy Fire, for example), and with every AOE melee skill with innate AOE. So for example, if you think of a fury druid: Fury will double ping the Enchant attack damage on its main target, and deal it once on the splash charm aoe and thats it. Now, a Shockwave druid will deal that + also deal its entire enchant damage on the Shockwave aoe.

That happens for other skills with innate aoe that carries elemental damage too, like Tectonic Slam, for example. Or Vengeance. Or Carnage. Or Impale. (Sacrifice AOE doesnt carry any elemental damage though if I understood the skill correctly. Correct me if wrong please.)

You can use this to levarage elemental damage on several melee builds. They will deal multiple times more damage than what is shown on character sheet page.

 

10.2. SUMMON WOLF

Summon wolves are particular regarding most summons in that it has imbued in its damage calculations the caster’s %fire and cold skill damage.

As far as I understood checking the files, that just straight multiply the numbers of the wolves normal attack and CTC spells, and not actually give the wolves %elemental damage modifiers (differently from -enemy res, which they actually inherit the stat). 

That basically means that when they are fire enchanted by your skill, they dont multiply those values again like the player would as explained on the last chapter - they enchant will affect them only by what is written on the source's tooltip.

Still, %elemental damage is beneficial for them in that way and its the reason approaching even a zoo druid as an elemental caster instead of just +ask stacking is better. Just wanted to mention it somewhere in case someone didn’t know.

 

10.3. FISSURE CTC PROC

There is one CTC spell that affects the build output, which is the lvl 30 Fissure.

The spell is a regular druid arsenal spell. It applies an effect on the ground and deals averaged fire damage on stuff above it. It is particular in that it doesnt stack on the tiles it affect, so even multiple procs wont deal more damage to stuff staying above it – the most noticeable effect of multiple procs is covering a big área of effect as they will radiate from any mob that triggers it in the Shockwave aoe.

All that means that its not a big contribution to your damage output, but it is basically free damage since Earth Shifter would still be the de-facto weapon for this build even without the proc.

The numbers for a level 30 Fissure with the gear presented in the guide are around 5k avarage fire damage per second.
